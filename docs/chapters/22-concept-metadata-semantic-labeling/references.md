# References: Concept Metadata and Semantic Labeling Standards

1. [Simple Knowledge Organization System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simple_Knowledge_Organization_System) - Wikipedia - Covers SKOS's preferred label, alternate label, and broader/narrower/related relation types in full, directly underlying this chapter's SKOS sections.

2. [SKOS Simple Knowledge Organization System Reference](https://www.w3.org/TR/skos-reference/) - W3C - The official W3C SKOS specification defining `skos:prefLabel`, `skos:altLabel`, `skos:broader`, `skos:narrower`, and `skos:related`, the formal source this chapter's SKOS relation sections summarize in plain language.

3. [Dublin Core](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dublin_Core) - Wikipedia - Explains the Dublin Core Metadata Element Set, including Title and Description, the standard this chapter's Dublin Core Title and Dublin Core Description sections apply to this project's own `metadata.json`.

4. [Unique identifier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unique_identifier) - Wikipedia - Covers the general design principles behind stable identifiers that outlive a changing display label, background for this chapter's Unique Concept Identifier section.

5. [Granularity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Granularity) - Wikipedia - Explains granularity as a level-of-detail concept applicable to information systems, the general principle this chapter's Concept Granularity, Atomic Concept, and Composite Concept sections apply specifically to concept labeling.

6. [Synonym ring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synonym_ring) - Wikipedia - Describes how controlled vocabularies group equivalent terms together, the information-science background for this chapter's Synonym Management section.

7. [Word-sense disambiguation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Word-sense_disambiguation) - Wikipedia - Covers the general problem of resolving which meaning a word carries in context, background for this chapter's Homonym Disambiguation section.

8. [Data governance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_governance) - Wikipedia - Surveys policies and practices for maintaining data quality and consistency over time, the broader discipline this chapter's Vocabulary Governance section narrows to concept labels specifically.

9. [Provenance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Provenance) - Wikipedia - Explains provenance as a chronology of ownership and origin, the general concept this chapter's Concept Provenance section applies to individual concept nodes in a learning graph.

10. ISO/IEC 11179 - Information technology — Metadata registries (MDR) - International Organization for Standardization - The metadata-registry standard this project's own Chapter 2 already cites for concept-definition conventions, extended here to naming-convention and machine-readable-metadata governance practices.
