# References: Bloom's Taxonomy and Learning Objectives

1. [Bloom's Taxonomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bloom%27s_taxonomy) - Wikipedia - Surveys the 1956 original and 2001 revised frameworks, the three domains, and the six cognitive levels from Remembering to Creating, covering the chapter's central classification system in full.

2. [Psychomotor Learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychomotor_learning) - Wikipedia - Explains the developmental stages of physical, hands-on skill acquisition, giving depth to the Psychomotor Domain that this chapter names but treats briefly relative to the cognitive domain.

3. [Educational Aims and Objectives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Educational_aims_and_objectives) - Wikipedia - Distinguishes broad educational aims from specific, measurable objectives and connects them to learning outcomes and assessment alignment, the same Bloom-Verb-plus-concept formula this chapter teaches.

4. A Taxonomy for Learning, Teaching, and Assessing: A Revision of Bloom's Taxonomy of Educational Objectives - Lorin W. Anderson and David R. Krathwohl (Eds.) - Addison Wesley Longman - The definitive source for the 2001 revision this chapter uses throughout, including the shift to active verbs and the reordering of Evaluating and Creating.

5. Taxonomy of Educational Objectives: The Classification of Educational Goals, Handbook I: Cognitive Domain - Benjamin S. Bloom (Ed.) - David McKay Company - The original 1956 framework this chapter contrasts with the 2001 revision, useful for seeing the noun-based level names the revision later replaced.

6. [Bloom's Taxonomy of Educational Objectives](https://www.cmu.edu/teaching/designteach/design/bloomsTaxonomy.html) - Carnegie Mellon University Eberly Center - Explains how to select action verbs at each cognitive level when designing course objectives, directly supporting this chapter's Bloom-Verb-plus-concept objective formula.

7. [Bloom's Taxonomy](https://celt.iastate.edu/prepare-and-teach/design-your-course/blooms-taxonomy/) - Iowa State University Center for Excellence in Learning and Teaching - Describes how the revised taxonomy's six levels guide course design from foundational recall tasks to advanced synthesis tasks, reinforcing this chapter's pyramid structure.

8. [Bloom's Taxonomy Tip Sheet](https://uwaterloo.ca/centre-for-teaching-excellence/catalogs/tip-sheets/blooms-taxonomy) - University of Waterloo Centre for Teaching Excellence - Summarizes the three domains and provides verb lists for each cognitive level, matching this chapter's own verb tables for each of the six levels.

9. [Using Bloom's Taxonomy to Write Effective Learning Objectives](https://tips.uark.edu/using-blooms-taxonomy/) - University of Arkansas Teaching Innovation and Pedagogical Support - Walks through pairing a Bloom-level verb with course content to produce a measurable objective, the exact construction practiced in this chapter's Learning Objective Builder MicroSim.

10. [Learning Objective Tip Sheet](https://cetl.uconn.edu/learning-objective-tip-sheet/) - University of Connecticut Center for Excellence in Teaching and Learning - Introduces the audience-behavior-condition-degree model for writing measurable objectives, complementing this chapter's simpler verb-plus-concept formula with a more detailed structure.
